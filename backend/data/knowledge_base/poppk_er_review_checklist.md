# PopPK/ER Evidence Review Knowledge Base

A PopPK review should examine the data sources, analysis population, concentration-time data, BLQ handling, structural model, random-effects model, residual error model, covariate search strategy, final parameter estimates, model diagnostics, VPC or pcVPC, bootstrap or uncertainty assessment, external validation where available, and simulation assumptions.

A dose justification package should connect the clinical question to evidence. The reviewer should identify the proposed dose, target population, exposure metric, exposure-response evidence, safety margin, special population assessment, simulation design, uncertainty propagation, and remaining limitations.

For covariates, distinguish statistical significance from clinical relevance. A covariate can be statistically significant without requiring dose adjustment. The key question is whether the exposure difference changes benefit-risk within the relevant exposure-response range.

For exposure-response, assess the endpoint definition, exposure metric selection, timing of exposure, model type, covariate adjustment, uncertainty intervals, event counts, and sensitivity analyses. Early exposure metrics may reduce response-driven bias for drugs with time-varying clearance.

For simulation, assess whether the simulated population matches the target population, whether covariate distributions are realistic, whether parameter uncertainty is propagated, whether results exceed the observed training range, and whether graphical outputs support the written conclusion.

Regulatory risk increases when conclusions are expressed more strongly than the underlying evidence, especially for special populations, high body weight extrapolation, ethnic sensitivity, ADA impact, dose adjustment, and label recommendations.
